# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests. This type of fraud usually happens through phone calls, fake messages, or phishing links.

In a typical scenario, a victim receives a call from someone pretending to be a bank official or a customer. The fraudster asks the victim to click on a link or approve a “collect request” on a UPI app. Believing it to be genuine, the victim enters their UPI PIN, which results in money being transferred from their account.

College students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not fully understand how digital payments work or may trust such calls easily. Fraudsters take advantage of urgency and fear, such as claiming that the bank account will be blocked.

The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems. In many cases, victims are unable to recover their money.

This shows the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment systems. Users should never share their UPI PIN or approve unknown requests.
